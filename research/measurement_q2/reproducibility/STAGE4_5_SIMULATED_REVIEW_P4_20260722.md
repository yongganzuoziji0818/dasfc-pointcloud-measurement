# Stage 4.5 simulated pre-submission review — P4 revision

## Review setup

- **Input scope:** complete anonymous manuscript, supplementary methods, eight figures, three tables, P0–P4 evidence receipts, claim ledger, reference audit, and submission QA.
- **Assessment boundary:** reviewer-style pressure test for a Measurement submission using Nature-style axes for originality, significance, technical soundness, cross-field interest, and readability. This is not an editorial decision and does not assume access to confidential reviews.
- **Shared manuscript claim summary:** DAS-FC aligns conformal calibration with the delivered scan-pair displacement-field event, producing simultaneous full-field coverage intervals under predefined synthetic groups while exposing fallback and out-of-domain status. The point estimator is fixed across interval methods.
- **Visible evidence base:** 240 held-out known-domain synthetic pairs; negative pointwise control; raw-local, homoscedastic, learned-unshrunk, pooled, max-t, and other frozen comparators; four grids; unseen and out-of-basis stresses; 120 repeated allocations; three registration front ends; deterministic rerun; public three-device scans; SHREC'19 real-deformation stress tests; and three Rockfall scan-pair events with four nested TS60 references, applicability diagnostics, and reference-tolerance sensitivity.
- **Missing materials affecting confidence:** no dense, independently measured real double-epoch displacement field; only three Rockfall events and four targets per event; no sound-barrier deployment data. These absences are stated in the manuscript.

## Reviewer 1

- **Overall assessment:** The technical chain is unusually disciplined for a simulation-led measurement-method paper. The complete-field event is defined before testing; data roles are separated; point estimates are held fixed; failed and negative evidence is retained; and the unit of inference is the scan pair. The P4 revision materially improves the paper by showing, using observable diagnostics, that Rockfall is outside the synthetic calibration envelope and by demonstrating that conservative reference tolerances do not reverse the finite three-event comparison.
- **Who would be interested in the results, and why:** researchers in point-cloud metrology, structural monitoring, spatial uncertainty, conformal prediction, and dense registration would care because the paper demonstrates that apparently satisfactory pointwise coverage can be nearly useless for a delivered field.
- **Major strengths:** clean attribution to the uncertainty layer; full-field rather than location-level calibration; independent tuning/validation/calibration roles; paired and group-aware statistics; explicit failed ablations; deterministic reproduction; and exceptionally clear separation between formal synthetic evidence and bounded physical corroboration.
- **Major concerns:** the finite-sample guarantee is established only for represented synthetic groups. Rockfall does not validate dense displacement coverage, and the physical sample is too small for population inference. The composition of known conformal ingredients may still be judged incremental unless the event-alignment contribution is read as the primary advance.
- **Technical failings that need to be addressed before the case is established:** no new pre-submission experiment is indispensable if the claim remains exactly as bounded. Any statement implying real-domain nominal coverage, dense physical validation, a complete GUM budget, or deployment readiness would be unsupported. The current manuscript avoids those statements.
- **Assessment against Nature-style criteria:** originality is moderate-to-strong at the level of measurement-event formulation and protocol composition; scientific importance is strong within dense measurement but not demonstrated as universal; interdisciplinary interest is credible for structured-output uncertainty; technical soundness is strong for the synthetic claim and bounded for real data; readability is good despite methodological density.
- **Recommendation posture:** supportive of external review in the stated simulation-led scope; a later request for denser real reference validation would be scientifically understandable but is not logically required to assess the bounded claim.

## Reviewer 2

- **Overall assessment:** The paper's most original result is not a new registration backbone but the alignment of the calibration unit, test unit, and delivered coverage event. The 0.946 pointwise coverage versus 2/240 complete-field coverage contrast is arresting and transferable. Proposition 1 and the comparator structure make the novelty easier to distinguish from generic conformal prediction.
- **Who would be interested in the results, and why:** readers working on spatial fields, imaging, deformation monitoring, uncertainty-aware digital twins, and other structured measurements may recognize the same marginal-versus-simultaneous failure mode.
- **Major strengths:** a clear negative control; direct learned-unshrunk and classical comparator bands; validation-only shrinkage; predefined domains and fallback; applicability diagnostics; and honest reporting when Cascade-Strong loses to classical front ends on public or Rockfall data.
- **Major concerns:** the novelty remains compositional. Several ingredients—group conditioning, max-score calibration, heteroscedastic scaling, fallback, and abstention—have precedents. The paper must therefore continue to foreground the new measurand-aligned interaction and not describe each component as independently novel. The absence of dense real truth reduces the demonstrated practical significance.
- **Technical failings that need to be addressed before the case is established:** the manuscript should not promote the deterministic field illustration into prevalence evidence, nor treat P2 reallocations as independent replications. These boundaries are already explicit. The target-level Table 3 should remain conceptual rather than be read as an empirical superiority table.
- **Assessment against Nature-style criteria:** originality is credible but compositional; scientific importance is strongest as a correction to evaluation practice; interdisciplinary reach is plausible because simultaneous field coverage recurs beyond point clouds; technical soundness is high for the frozen protocol; readability is improved by the workflow schematic and field-level failure example.
- **Recommendation posture:** promising and suitable for review at a specialist measurement journal; final enthusiasm will depend on whether the editor values a rigorous evaluation target and protocol as a sufficient measurement-science advance.

## Reviewer 3

- **Overall assessment:** The revision is readable for a technically sophisticated nonspecialist. The abstract now states the central contrast, physical evidence, and limitation without forcing the reader to discover the boundary in the discussion. Figures 1 and 8 make the workflow, out-of-domain diagnosis, and full-field failure visible.
- **Who would be interested in the results, and why:** measurement scientists outside point-cloud registration may find the paper useful as a case study in defining the correct coverage event for correlated high-dimensional outputs.
- **Major strengths:** clear measurand language; careful separation from VIM/GUM uncertainty terminology; concise evidence hierarchy; captions that say what each figure cannot establish; and a discussion organized around event alignment, shift, and physical corroboration rather than algorithm promotion.
- **Major concerns:** the main paper remains long and acronym-dense. Readers may still conflate simultaneous scalar-component intervals with calibrated 3-D vector regions unless they attend to the boundary language. The practical consequence of wider full-field intervals should be kept visible when interpreting coverage.
- **Technical failings that need to be addressed before the case is established:** no readability defect blocks submission. The title, abstract, cover letter, and conclusion must remain synchronized with the simulation-led scope; any later metadata or repository update should not alter scientific wording without rebuilding and rechecking the anonymous PDF.
- **Assessment against Nature-style criteria:** originality and significance are understandable from the pointwise/full-field contrast; interdisciplinary interest is plausible but not proven by applications outside point clouds; technical soundness is clearly bounded; readability is above average for the topic.
- **Recommendation posture:** supportive of submission after final archive/version synchronization; further compression would now risk deleting necessary scope boundaries.

## Cross-review synthesis

- **Consensus strengths:** correct independent unit; fixed point estimates; strong negative control; explicit guarantee conditions; multiple frozen robustness checks; transparent failures; reproducible evidence; and unusually disciplined separation of synthetic confirmation from physical corroboration.
- **Consensus technical risks:** no dense independently measured real displacement field; small Rockfall event count; compositional novelty; and the possibility that a reviewer treats physical validation as mandatory for Measurement despite the narrowed claim.
- **Where emphasis differs across reviewers:** Reviewer 1 places greatest weight on technical validity and concludes the bounded synthetic case is established. Reviewer 2 places greatest weight on originality and views the measurement-event formulation as essential to overcoming the incremental-combination criticism. Reviewer 3 places greatest weight on readability and warns against either acronym expansion or deleting the remaining boundary language.
- **Broad-interest / significance readout:** the strongest transferable message is that marginal point coverage does not certify a delivered spatial field. The evidence demonstrates this convincingly in the tested point-cloud setting; broader scientific impact remains a plausible implication rather than an empirically demonstrated result.
- **Most important issues to resolve before a strong case is established:** no further scientific change is required for the present bounded submission. The remaining tasks are version/DOI synchronization, preservation of the anonymous-versus-title-page split, and a final hash manifest. Dense physical truth remains the highest-value future extension, not a claim that can be repaired by wording.

## Risk / unsupported claims

- Unsupported: nominal real-domain simultaneous coverage, dense physical displacement validation, calibrated 3-D vector regions, population Rockfall performance, sound-barrier deployment performance, universal frontend ranking, GUM expanded uncertainty, or metrological traceability.
- Supported only as descriptive or stress evidence: SHREC cross-resolution behavior, three-device disagreement, Rockfall 3/3 versus 0/3 finite-event counts, and the deterministically selected 624-location illustration.
- Not assessable from the supplied evidence: how a larger independent real double-epoch corpus would change coverage, efficiency, or frontend ordering.
